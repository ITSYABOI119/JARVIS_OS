=== UNANIMOUS FINDINGS (all 5 judges flagged) ===

1. tinyllama-1.1b: Non-functional. No answer on 7/10 prompts (echoed prompt,
   [end of text]). Degenerate repetition on P5. Falsely claims seL4 is "based
   on LLVM compiler infrastructure."

2. Qwen3.5-4B: Leaked <think> blocks on P2, P5, P9, P10. Visible chain-of-
   thought tokens consumed output budget, preventing actual answers. P5
   hallucinated a multiple-choice quiz format.

3. Qwen3-4B: Severe prompt expansion. Generated lists of follow-up questions
   instead of answers on P3, P6, P7, P8. P4 described constraints but produced
   no code. P5 initially claimed "Sydney" before correcting.

4. Llama-3.1-8B: Training data contamination. "PowerPoint PPT Presentation"
   headers on P3 and P6 (scraped SlideShare). "[duplicate]" tag on P8
   (scraped StackOverflow). Despite being 8B, scored below 3B Llama.

5. Phi-3-mini: Complete failure on P2 and P9 (echoed prompt only). Leaked
   <|assistant|> control tokens on P4, P5. Unreliable — good when it works,
   but 20% failure rate.

6. gemma-4-E4B: Generally strong but catastrophic P7 failure — leaked internal
   "Self-Correction/Review" planning notes, ended with "Proceed to generate
   the final response..." without ever producing the answer.

7. Llama-3.2-1B: P1 factual error — claims seL4 is "based on the Linux
   kernel." P10 missing .c filter, wrong size unit (1GB not 1MB).

8. gemma-4-E2B: Consistently strongest. Accurate, concise, well-structured on
   every prompt. No leaked tokens, no contamination. P5 was perfectly concise
   ("Canberra"). Code outputs correct.

9. mistral-7b: Most reliable. Zero failures, zero artifacts, zero
   contamination across all 10 prompts. Correctly identified capability-based
   access control (unique seL4 insight). Slightly verbose but never wrong.

10. Qwen3.5-9B: Strong quality with accurate answers, but tends to generate
    follow-up questions after answering (P1, P3, P5, P6, P8), wasting tokens.
    Excellent P9 (RISC/CISC). No leaked tokens unlike the 4B variant.

=== KEY DECISION POINTS ===

1. gemma-4-E2B is the unanimous #1 across all 5 judges with near-zero
   disagreement (sigma=0.04). It leads #2 by a full point (8.53 vs 7.57).

2. mistral-7b and gemma-4-E4B are effectively tied for #2/#3 (7.57 vs 7.56,
   0.01 difference). mistral-7b has zero failures; E4B has one catastrophic
   failure (P7). For reliability, mistral-7b wins.

3. Llama-3.1-8B (5.19) scored BELOW Llama-3.2-3B (5.98) despite being 2.5x
   larger. Training data contamination is the culprit. Disqualified.

4. Qwen3.5-9B (7.31) is strong but requires ~2000 LOC SSM engine work.
   Good future investment if Qwen architecture support is added.
